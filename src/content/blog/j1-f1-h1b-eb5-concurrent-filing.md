<intro>The United States has long been a primary destination for highly skilled professionals and ambitious students from India and China. However, the traditional employment-based immigration pathways, such as the EB-2 and EB-3 categories, have been categorized by severe backlogs that span decades. This structural delay creates persistent professional and personal instability for individuals residing in the United States on temporary nonimmigrant visas such as the H-1B visa, F-1 visa, and J-1 visa.

Additionally, passage of the EB-5 Reform and Integrity Act of 2022 fundamentally altered this landscape. By introducing concurrent filing of immigrant petitions and adjustment of status applications, the <a href="/research/post-reform-integrity-act-analysis">Reform and Integrity Act</a> established a mechanism for temporary visa holders to obtain immediate work and travel authorization, which effectively bypassed employer-dependent visa constraints. Indian and Chinese professionals on J-1, F-1, and H-1B visas can utilize the EB-5 program as a strategic pathway to permanent residency and efficiently bypass these decade-long backlogs in the process.</intro>

## Nonimmigrant visas vs EB-5 Program

Understanding the limitations of non-immigrant visas is essential to evaluating the utility of the EB-5 pathway. The H-1B, F-1, and J-1 visas are temporary, restrictive, and dependent on third-party sponsorship or strict regulatory compliance. In contrast the EB-5 program is a self-sponsored immigrant visa pathway based on capital investment.

## Backlogs, Layoffs and Restrictions

### The EB-2 and EB-3 Backlog Crisis

For Indian and Chinese nationals, the demand for EB-2 and EB-3 visas vastly exceeds the per-country statutory caps. According to the Congressional Research Service, the backlog for Indian nationals in the EB-2 and EB-3 categories is projected to result in wait times exceeding several decades, with some estimates lasting a lifetime for recent applicants.

Even though an H-1B holder can extend their status beyond the six-year limit if they have an approved I-140 petition, they remain locked in temporary status, unable to easily change employers or pursue entrepreneurial ventures. For a deeper look at this dynamic, see our analysis of <a href="/research/eb5-guide-indian-professionals-2026">EB-5 for Indian professionals</a>.

### Employer Dependence and Tech Layoffs

The legal status of an H-1B worker is contingent upon active employment. In the event of a layoff, termination, or corporate closure, the visa holder has a strict 60-day grace period to secure a new sponsoring employer, file for a change of status, or depart the country. During periods of economic contraction or industry-specific layoffs, finding an employer willing to sponsor an H-1B transfer within 60 days is practically impossible and exposes families to sudden displacement. Read more on how the <a href="/research/eb5-lifeline-h1b-workers">EB-5 serves as a lifeline for H-1B workers</a>.

### J-1 and F-1 Constraints

Most foreign medical graduates in the United States conduct their residency and fellowship training under J-1 exchange visitor status. This status carries a mandatory two-year home country physical presence requirement. To remain in the United States to practice medicine these physicians must secure a competitive J-1 waiver job, typically in a designated medically underserved area, and work under restrictive H-1B terms for three years before they can even get in the green card process.

The transition to a secure work visa is highly unpredictable for F-1 students. Optional practical training provides temporary work authorization for up to 12 months plus a 24-month extension for STEM graduates. Use our <a href="/tools/opt-calculator">OPT Calculator</a> to map your work authorization window before it expires. However long-term employment requires selection in the annual H-1B lottery, which has seen historically low selection rates due to massive demand. In 2025 there were over 470,000 registrations for only 85,000 available slots, representing a selection rate of less than 20%. F-1 students considering an investment-based alternative can explore our <a href="/research/f1-students/f1-to-eb5-green-card">F-1 to EB-5 green card guide</a>.

## How Students and Professionals can use concurrent filing

The EB-5 Reform and Integrity Act of 2022 introduced specific statutory provisions that directly address some of the vulnerabilities of non-immigrant visa holders.

### Concurrent Filing

Prior to the Reform and Integrity Act an EB-5 investor had to wait for the approval of their immigrant petition before they could apply for adjustment of status. The RIA changed this by permitting concurrent filing for applicants legally residing in the United States as long as a visa is immediately available in their category. When an investor concurrently files Form I-526E or Form I-485, they are also eligible to file Form I-765 for an employment authorization document and Form I-131 for advance parole.

### Interim benefits of concurrent filing

- Employment authorization provides unrestricted work authorization, which allows the applicant and their spouse to work for any U.S. employer, start a business, or work as an independent contractor. This eliminates dependence on H-1B or J-1 employer sponsorship and gives F-1 visa holders the freedom to work for who they choose after they graduate from their program.
- Advance parole serves as a travel document which enables international travel without the need to obtain a nonimmigrant visa stamp at a U.S. consulate abroad.

These interim benefits are typically approved within 4-8 months of filing, establishing an independent legal status in the United States while the underlying EB-5 petition is adjudicated.

## Strategic Recommendations for H-1B, J-1 and F-1 visas

For Indian and Chinese professionals on J-1, F-1, and H-1B visas, the Post-RIA EB-5 program represents a predictable self-sponsored alternative to decades-long employment backlogs. The ability to file concurrently provides immediate professional mobility, entrepreneurial freedom, and family stability within months of filing.
