<intro>The EB-5 Reform and Integrity Act of 2022 gave investors three concrete advantages: concurrent filing of I-526E and I-485 for immediate work and travel authorization, priority processing of rural TEA petitions in as little as 4–8 months, and mandatory I-956F project pre-approval before investor petitions are adjudicated. Together, these changes made EB-5 the fastest employment-based green card route for backlogged nationals from India and China — without employer sponsorship. The analysis below covers each provision, who benefits most, and what project due diligence looks like in the post-RIA era.</intro>

The 2022 enactment of the EB-5 Reform and Integrity Act has fundamentally reshaped the landscape of US investment immigration. The EB-5 has transitioned into a transparent and regulated program with investor safety being the main focus. The material improvements introduced by the RIA have made the EB-5 program much more attractive to prospective investors (rarely do governments make investment migration programs better!). This article will explore the improvements and the shifting demographics because of these improvements and the economic effect of redirected capital in this post RIA era. For a comprehensive overview, see our <a href="/research/complete-2027-eb5-guide">Complete 2027 EB-5 Guide</a>.

## The Legislative Framework

The RIA was designed to address long standing criticism regarding the lack of oversight of the EB-5 program and the concentration of capital in "mega cities". To combat these criticisms, the legislation introduced mandatory I-956F project applications which require USCIS to review the structure and documentation of a project before or alongside investor petitions. This ensures that the capital stack, business plan and job creation strategies are vetted early in the process. Additional integrity measures included are the appointment of fund administrators and the implementation of regular audits to deter bad actors and prevent the misuse of investor funds. These guardrails offer a level of security previously unseen in the program and provide assurance that regional centers are operating within strict regulatory boundaries.

## Economic Impact

One of the primary goals of the RIA has been the successful redistribution of investment into rural areas and second-tier cities. Data indicates that projects are now being launched in states that had never previously received EB-5 capital. Use our TEA Project Explorer to discover these opportunities. The economic contributions of the program are significant:

**Job Creation:** The program created over 1.7 million jobs for American communities between 2016 and 2019. Current data shows that the average investor creates 45 jobs which far exceeds the requirement of 10 jobs.

**Multiplier Effect:** EB-5 capital acts as a catalyst for broader investment. Historically every $500,000 in EB-5 funding added an additional $1.6m in private investment. Logically these numbers increase as EB-5 capital investment increases. This economic effect can be observed especially in rural regions where traditional financing is difficult to obtain.

**Wages:** The program has contributed over $122 billion in wages to American workers with the average salary being about $74,000 per job.

## EB-5 Investor Post RIA Profiles and Motivations

The RIA has ushered in a shift in investor demographics. Today's petitions have to be accredited investors and are sophisticated due to increased information sharing and direct communication with regional centers, attorneys and the internet of things. The shift in demographics can be seen in the following due to concurrent filing and increased investor protection measures:

**Domestic filers** are now a significant portion of the EB-5 market thanks to concurrent filing. These investors already reside in the United States and are on H-1B, F-1 (Student Visa), or L-1 visas.

**H-1B holders** that are trapped in decade long waits for other employment-based visas have benefited substantially from the RIA. The EB-5 now offers a faster route to permanent residency. Learn more about <a href="/research/5-reasons-switch-h1b-to-eb5">5 reasons to switch from H-1B to EB-5</a>.

## The Importance of Concurrent Filing and Rural Priority

The RIA added two key features that have emerged as bonafide game changers for the industry:

### Concurrent Filing (Adjustment of Status)

Investors already in the United States can file their I-485 adjustment of status concurrently with their EB-5 petition. This grants them interim benefits which include the employment authorization document (EAD) and the advanced parole travel permit. It's like the investor already received the green card because of the benefits rendered when applying for their adjustment of status. This allows <a href="/research/f1-students/f1-to-eb5-green-card">students to stay in the United States post-graduation</a> without needing employer sponsorship and enables professionals to change employers freely or start a business.

### Rural Priority Processing

The RIA mandates priority processing for investors in rural Targeted Employment Areas. While unemployment (urban) projects often face delays of 16 to 36 months, rural petitions are being approved in as little as 4 to 8 months. This speed has made rural projects the most popular choice for investors from backlogged countries like India and China who wish to leapfrog the traditional employment-based visa waiting times. ## Due Diligence

EB-5 is not a transaction to buy a green card like the proposed <a href="/research/eb5-vs-trump-gold-card-2026">Trump Gold Card</a> (the gold card is a donation, and the visas are allocated from other employment-based visa categories which can add individuals to backlogs). The EB-5 is a structured investment that needs to remain at risk throughout the process (this does not mean the investment must be risky!). Success depends on the project's performance, compliance and the team behind the project. For a detailed framework, read our guide on <a href="/research/19-questions-rural-eb5-project-due-diligence">19 Questions to Analyze an EB-5 Deal</a>.

A solid project should have its equity secured and a signed construction loan term sheet in place. Investors should also look at the developer track record to ensure the developer has completed similar projects on time and on budget and to confirm the economic logic the business plan is based on makes sense. A luxury hotel in a low demand area is high risk regardless of its EB-5 status. Lastly, the investor should determine if the developer has a clear strategy for capital recovery (refinance or sale) and be sure that it aligns with the required two year "at risk" period.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the current minimum for an EB-5 Visa?

The minimum investment is $800,000 for Targeted Employment Areas (TEAs) which include rural areas and regions with high unemployment. The minimum for projects outside of these areas is $1,050,000.

### Can I get a work permit while my green card is pending?

Yes. Someone already in the United States on another visa can apply for Employment Authorization Document (EAD) and Advance Parole (AP) for travel simultaneously with their EB-5 application thanks to concurrent filing. This is described as interim benefits which give the investor the benefits of having the green card before they actually get the green card.

### How long does the EB-5 process take?

Timelines vary by category. Rural projects receive priority processing and approvals can be seen in 6 to 11 months. High unemployment projects typically take longer.

### What is the difference between regional center and direct investment models?

Direct Investment requires the investor to manage the business and only direct payroll (W-2) jobs count towards the fulfillment of the job requirement. The regional center investment route is passive, and the project can count indirect and induced jobs (like construction) towards the ten job requirement.

### What happens if the project I invest in fails?

EB-5 capital must remain "at risk". If a project fails to create the required jobs or goes bankrupt you could lose the capital as well as the path to a green card. This is why due diligence on the developer and business plan is critical.

### What is grandfathering?

Petitions filed before September 30, 2026 are grandfathered meaning they will continue to be processed according to current rules even if the EB-5 law expires or is changed by congress.
