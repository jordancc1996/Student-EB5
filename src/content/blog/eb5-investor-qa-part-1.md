<intro>The EB-5 program offers foreign nationals permanent residency (green card) in exchange for a capital investment into a U.S. commercial enterprise that creates 10 jobs for U.S. workers. The investment amount for the EB-5 program is $800,000 for projects located in a Targeted Employment Area (Rural and High Unemployment Areas) or $1,050,000 outside of these areas. Navigating the EB-5 program requires a clear understanding of investment structures, processing timelines, regulatory deadlines and the EB-5 landscape; all of which we address in the following article.</intro>

## How do I choose an EB-5 project?

Choosing an EB-5 project requires substantial due diligence and an understanding that the ROI of EB-5 is the green card and not investment returns. There is no single project that offers the fastest immigration processing times, shortest repayment timeline and a high financial return. Projects located in rural areas receive priority processing times from USCIS which makes them the most efficient option for investors seeking a green card quickly. This speed is advantageous for investors born in countries like India and China who experience substantial backlogs.

High unemployment area projects do not have priority processing but are at less of a retrogression risk than rural projects and offer different financial structures. Investors should evaluate their risk tolerance, need for capital and their urgency for a green card before selecting a project. The most important aspect of the due diligence period for an investor should be analyzing the regional center they decide to go with. Does this regional center have a good track record? Is the capital stack healthy? Do they have I-956F approval for their project? These are some of the questions that need to be answered before a wire is transferred.

## Why are rural EB-5 projects processing faster than high unemployment projects?

Rural EB-5 projects process faster because the EB-5 Reform and Integrity act of 2022 mandates priority processing for petitions associated with rural investments. An investor in a rural project can generally expect their I-526E petition to be approved in four to nine months. For an HUA project, the processing time for the same petition could be as long as 18 to 24 months.

## How long does it take to get a green card after filing an EB-5 application?

For a rural project, the I-526E petition is typically approved in 4 to 9 months and after approval it takes an additional 6 to 10 months to complete the adjustment of status or consular processing and receive the physical green card. This places the total timeline for a rural project at approximately 12 to 18 months from filing to receive the conditional green card. For an HUA project, the I-526E petition takes 18 to 24 months to be approved. Adding the six to ten months for final processing and the total timelines are estimated at two and a half to three years.

## How long will my money be tied up in an EB-5 investment?

The repayment timeline is dictated by the loan documents between the EB-5 regional center and the project developer. Investors must look at the maximum allowable term of the loan, including all available extensions. As far as the EB-5 investment goes, the minimum allowable timeline an investors capital must remain "at risk" in an approved project is two years which begins on the date the capital is fully invested and placed at risk with the job creating entity.

## What is the EB-5 grandfathering clause and how does it protect investors?

The grandfathering clause was introduced by the EB-5 reform and Integrity Act of 2022 and protects investors from future legislative changes or program expirations. Any investor who files their I-526E petition before September 30, 2026, is grandfathered into the program. Even if congress fails to reauthorize the regional center program or changes the investment requirements after that date, the grandfathered applications will be processed under the rules in place at the time of filing and ensure the investor remains eligible for their green card.

## Can I transition from an L-1B visa to EB-5 and stay in the United States?

An investor currently in the U.S. on a valid nonimmigrant visa (including H-1B, L-1B) can file for an EB-5 visa and submit an adjustment of status application concurrently as long as their visa category is current on the visa bulletin.

Filing the adjustment of status grants the investor a new "adjustment pending" status which exists alongside their current nonimmigrant status. The investor also applies for an Employment authorization document to be able to work freely and advance parole for travel. The investor can remain in the United States based on their adjustment pending status.

## How much time is needed to prepare an EB-5 source of funds application?

The first step in the EB-5 process is hiring an immigration attorney. The attorney will guide the investor on how to consolidate funds in compliance with USCIS regulations. Documenting the lawful source and path of funds requires assembling years of tax returns, bank statements, property records and employment history. Even for straight forward cases, it may take an experienced attorney four to six weeks to organize and gather all necessary documentation. During this period, the investor can evaluate projects. Once the documentation is complete and the project is selected the investor can file their petition.
