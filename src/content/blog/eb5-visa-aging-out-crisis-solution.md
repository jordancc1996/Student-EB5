The current American immigration system that was designed to attract and retain global talent has paradoxically created a profound crisis for the children and spouses of skilled immigrants. The crisis known as aging out affects dependents of nonimmigrant visas. Upon turning 21, these children lose their dependent status and their eligibility to immigrate with their family due to decades long visa backlogs. While temporary fixes exist like the F-1 student visa it is not a permanent fix and merely delays the inevitable. <a href="/research/5-reasons-switch-h1b-to-eb5">The most certain, accelerated and strategically sound solution to this dilemma is the EB-5 immigrant investor visa</a>. The EB-5 visa allows the primary investor, their spouse and unmarried children (under the age of 21) to apply for a green card. <a href="/research/how-eb5-visa-transforms-us-education-experience">The program offers a direct and often backlog free path to a green card and eliminates the need for families to seek residency abroad</a>. ## Backlogs and the Failure of the CSPA

A parent on an H-1B visa typically files an I-140 Immigrant Petition for Alien workers to establish a priority date for the entire family. The Child Status Protection Act (CSPA) was intended to protect children from aging out due to slow processing times by "freezing" their age based on a complex formula. The formula subtracts the time the I-140 petition as pending from the Childs age on the date the visa becomes available. However, for backlogged countries the CSPA is often rendered useless. The visa "becomes available" only when the priority date is current and in the case of a 10–20-year backlog it may not "be current" until it is too late. The dependent is then forced to seek an independent non-immigrant status visa like the F-1 or lose their place in the family's Green Card queue.

## Emigration to Foreign Countries

The sheer hopelessness of the employment-based queue has driven many highly skilled H-1B families to consider drastic alternatives including <a href="https://www.retirementcitizenship.com/blogs/h1b-migration-portugal" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">emigration from the United States</a>. Families actively explore foreign residency programs like Golden Visas despite wanting to stay in the United States. While these foreign options offer a path to stability and citizenship elsewhere, they fundamentally fail to solve the family's core problem which is securing a future in the United States. For families whose careers, social lives and primary goals are centered in the U.S., leaving the country is a last resort. The EB-5 visa offers the only direct and certain solution that allows the family to remain together and achieve permanent residency in the U.S.

## The EB-5 Visa: A Strategic Investment in Certainty

The EB-5 investment visa grants a Green Card in exchange for a qualifying investment that creates 10 U.S. jobs. The Eb-5 offers a unique combination of benefits that make it the most effect for families facing the aging out crisis:

### The Power of the EB-5 Reform and Integrity Act (RIA) 2022

The set-aside categories are a game changer for investors from backlogged countries like India and China. The demand for these reserved visas is currently lower than the supply and the priority dates for these categories are often current on the visa bulletin. This means that once the initial I-526 petition is approved a visa is immediately available. This eliminates the decades-long wait that plagues the H-1B to green card pathway.

### CSPA Protection and Concurrent Filing

The EB-5 path also offers superior CSPA protection. The CSPA (Child Status Protection Act) is a law that protects children from aging out (turning 21) and becoming ineligible for a green card during the long processing time of the investors' petition. By filing the I-526 petition the family locks in the child's CSPA age. For those utilizing a current set-aside category the family can often file the I-526 and the I-485 adjustment of status petition concurrently.

The ability to file the I-485 immediately is the key to solving the aging-out problem for dependents. Once the I-485 is filed the child is projected from aging out regardless of how long the processing takes. Furthermore, within a few months of filing the I-485, the child can receive an Employment Authorization Document (EAD) and Advance Parole. This provides immediate stability for the dependent and allows the dependent to work and travel internationally while they continue their life in the United States without the fear of losing status or the need to maintain an F-1 visa.

The EB-5 immigrant investor program is the most effective and direct solution for H-1B families facing the tragedy of their children aging out. By leveraging the set-side visa categories established by the RIA families can bypass the crippling backlogs of the Employment based system and secure a Green Card for their children with speed and certainty. ## Key Terms

I-526 Petition: This is the initial application for an investor to receive a conditional green card through the EB-5 program.

Set Asides: These are reserved visa categories within the U.S. Eb-5 Immigrant Investor Program.

Visa Bulletin: The visa bulletin is a monthly publication by the United States that shows the availability of immigrant visas.

Concurrent Filing: Allows individuals already in the United States to submit their Immigrant Petition for Alien Worker (I-526) and application to adjust status (I-485) at the same time. Concurrent filing allows the individual to get the benefits of the green card before getting the green card.

Golden Visa: Golden Visa grants foreign nationals temporary or permanent residency in a country in exchange for a significant financial investment.

I-485 Adjustment of Status: The adjustment of status allows eligible foreign nationals to become permanent residents without have to return to their home country to complete visa processing.

CSPA: Child Status Protection Act is a law that helps prevent children from aging out of eligibility for lawful permanent residency due to government processing delays.

Employment Authorization Document: Document that allows foreign nationals to legally work in the United States.

Advance Parole: Document that allows foreign nationals to travel outside the United States and return without a visa.
