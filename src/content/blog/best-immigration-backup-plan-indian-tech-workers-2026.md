<intro>Recent years have been filled with trials and tribulations for Indian tech workers in the United States. Inherent uncertainties tied to the H-1B visa program combined with layoffs and prolonged green card backlogs have made the EB-5 program stand out as a strategic and effective solution for tech employees. The EB-5 program offers a direct and predictable pathway to U.S. permanent residency and effectively circumvents the decades-long waits associated with other employment-based categories all while providing a critical layer of stability and freedom to the investor. This analysis discusses the importance of acting urgently in the upcoming months for Indian tech employees who want to stay in the United States.</intro>

## The H-1B Predicament: Uncertainty and Insecurity

The H-1B visa carries inherent limitations for the visa holder and these limitations generate instability and insecurity. The H-1B visa is a temporary non-immigrant visa and an individual's legal status is contingent upon employment with a sponsoring company. This dependency creates a precarious situation for the visa holder especially in times of economic volatility or industry-specific downturns like the <a href="/research/h1b-tech-layoffs-eb5-entrepreneurship">tech layoffs we are experiencing now</a>. When employment ends, H-1B workers face a strict 60-day grace period to secure new sponsorship or change their immigration status or risk deportation.

Indian nationals on H-1B visas experience extensive backlogs in other employment-based green card categories like EB-2 and EB-3. These backlogs are driven by higher demand that exceeds per-country annual limits and typically extend for more than a decade, with some estimates for new Indian EB-2 applicants reaching 20 to 30+ years. H-1B workers and their families remain in a temporary status during this prolonged waiting period which typically restricts career progression, limits salary negotiation power and impedes long-term personal planning. H-1B workers earn 20% less on average than their American counterparts for similar roles because of the structural limitations and reduced job mobility imposed by their visa status. This long-term uncertainty and lack of control over an H-1B's immigration future are the primary drivers we are seeing for the increasing interest in the EB-5 program today.

## EB-5 as THE Strategic Solution

The EB-5 immigrant investor program offers a distinct alternative by providing a direct path to a green card based on capital investment in the U.S. economy. The EB-5 program is self-sponsored and removes the dependence on an employer. The Reform and Integrity Act of 2022 (RIA) significantly enhanced the program's appeal with concurrent filing which made it much more attractive to individuals already residing in the U.S. on H-1B visas. For a deeper breakdown of the law, see our <a href="/research/eb5-reform-integrity-act-2022-analysis">RIA analysis</a>.

One of the most revolutionary developments introduced by the RIA is the ability for a U.S.-based investor to file their I-526E immigration petition concurrently with their I-485 application to register permanent residence and adjust status. This concurrent filing mechanism allows applicants to secure interim benefits including an Employment Authorization Document (EAD) and Advance Parole, typically within 4 to 8 months. The EAD provides unrestricted work authorization and frees H-1B holders from sponsorship constraints, which can help the investor work for whomever they choose, start their own businesses or work as independent contractors. Advance Parole permits international travel without abandoning the adjustment of status application which addresses one of the significant limitations of the H-1B visa. This stability is particularly crucial for those facing tech layoffs or job insecurity as it provides a safety net that allows them to remain in the U.S. and seek new employment under their pending adjustment of status.

The EB-5 visa extends stability to the entire family even beyond the individual investor. The investor's spouse and unmarried children under 21 are eligible for green cards, allowing them to live, work and study in the United States without restriction. Spouses gain work authorization and children can benefit from in-state tuition rates at public universities which can lead to enhanced education and career opportunities and greater access to financial aid. This comprehensive family stability is a significant advantage over other visa categories.

The financial profiles of many tech workers on H-1B visas finally align well with the current EB-5 investment requirements. Years of high salaries, accumulated restricted stock units and real estate ownership often mean these professionals have the necessary capital or access to it through various funding mechanisms. The $800,000 investment threshold for targeted employment areas is often achievable for individuals who have spent a decade earning senior-level salaries at major tech companies. Flexibility in sourcing funds makes the EB-5 program even more accessible and allows for strategic <a href="/research/h1b-eb5-financial-planning">financial planning</a> for the best outcome for the investor. For a detailed look at funding methods, see our <a href="/research/ways-to-fund-eb5-investment-2026">guide to funding an EB-5 investment in 2026</a>.

## Understanding the Basics of the EB-5 Program

The EB-5 program requires a foreign national to invest capital in a new commercial enterprise that creates or preserves at least 10 full-time jobs for qualifying U.S. workers. The standard investment amount is $1,050,000 in non-TEA projects and $800,000 for projects located in Targeted Employment Areas, which include rural areas or areas experiencing high unemployment. A core principle of the EB-5 program is that the investment capital must be considered "at risk," meaning it is subject to both potential gain and loss. Investors must provide meticulous documentation proving that the investment funds were obtained from lawful sources.

Most EB-5 investors opt to pool their funds in projects managed by USCIS-designated Regional Centers. A regional center is an entity that facilitates EB-5 investments within a defined geographic area, often managing projects that meet the TEA criteria. This approach simplifies the investment process for individuals and leverages the expertise of experienced project developers. Our <a href="/research/choosing-rural-eb5-regional-center-guide">regional center selection guide</a> walks through how to evaluate these projects.

## EB-5 Application for U.S.-Based Applicants

The EB-5 process can be initiated while maintaining current nonimmigrant status for H-1B holders currently residing in the United States. The general steps are as follows:

1. **Investment:** The investor conducts due diligence to select a suitable EB-5 project, typically within a regional center, and makes the required capital investment into the new commercial enterprise.

2. **Filing of Form I-526E:** The investor files Form I-526E, Immigrant Petition by Regional Center Investor, with USCIS. This petition meticulously documents the investment and provides a detailed business plan outlining how the job creation requirement will be met.

3. **Concurrent Filing of Form I-485 (Adjustment of Status):** A significant benefit introduced by the RIA is the ability to file Form I-485, Application to Register Permanent Residence or Adjust Status, concurrently with the I-526E petition. This allows the applicant and their dependent family members to remain in the United States under a period of authorized stay while the EB-5 petition is adjudicated.

4. **Interim Benefits (EAD/AP):** When filing Form I-485, applicants can also file Form I-765 for an Employment Authorization Document (EAD) and Form I-131 for an Advance Parole travel document. Once approved, the EAD grants unrestricted work authorization and gives the H-1B holder freedom from sponsor dependence. Advance Parole allows international travel without abandoning the adjustment of status application.

5. **Petition to Remove Conditions (Form I-829):** Within the 90-day period before the conditional green card expires, typically two years after I-485 approval, the investor must file Form I-829 to remove the conditions on their residency. Upon approval of the I-829, the investor, their spouse and their children receive permanent green cards.

## Present and Future Risks of EB-5

Rural projects benefit from a larger visa allocation of 20% and receive priority processing from USCIS. The high unemployment area category has a smaller allocation of 10%, and industry experts anticipate that rural projects may experience backlogs first due to their popularity while high unemployment areas could eventually face longer backlogs if adjudication remains slow. Retrogression should be a key consideration but not the sole factor in deciding which project to invest in. Investors should qualify a project based on the quality of the <a href="/research/eb5-capital-stack">capital stack</a>, the regional center and their own personal risk tolerance.

A critical deadline for prospective investors is September 30, 2026. The RIA included a <a href="/research/eb5-grandfathering-explained">grandfathering provision</a> that protects investors who file their I-526E petitions on or before this date. This provision ensures that petitions filed by this date will continue to be processed by USCIS even if the Regional Center Program is not reauthorized beyond its current expiration date of September 30, 2027. Filing after this date exposes investors to significant regulatory and political risk as the investment amount will likely increase to stay on pace with inflation on January 1, 2027.

Another important consideration for families is the Child Status Protection Act (CSPA). USCIS updated its CSPA age calculation policy in August 2025, reverting to a more restricted interpretation that uses only the final action date for CSPA age calculations. This change has significant implications for EB-5 families particularly those from countries like India where visa backlogs are expected to develop. The correlation between the grandfathering deadline, potential retrogression and CSPA protection underscores the complexity and the importance of acting quickly.

## The Future of EB-5

Concerns about the program's long-term future are understandable. The consensus within the EB-5 community is that the program will be re-authorized under different terms due to its documented positive economic impact. The program has generated billions of dollars in capital investment and supported the creation of hundreds of thousands of jobs for U.S. workers. Between 2016 and 2019 the EB-5 program created an estimated 1.7 million American jobs and since the RIA passage in 2022 the program has brought over $5.36 billion into the U.S. economy.

It is widely anticipated that the program will become more expensive and stringent in the future. The investment amount is projected to increase with inflation starting in 2027, with estimates placing the new standard at approximately $920,000 for TEA projects. Future legislation is also likely to include more robust integrity and anti-money laundering measures such as more rigorous KYC checks and OFAC/SDN screening requirements.

## Conclusion

For tech workers in the USA grappling with H-1B uncertainty and prolonged green card queues, the EB-5 immigrant investor program offers a compelling value proposition. The EB-5 is the only program that offers a direct, self-sponsored pathway to U.S. permanent residency, granting unparalleled stability, career freedom and family security. The reforms introduced by the RIA, particularly concurrent filing, have significantly enhanced its appeal, offering interim benefits that mitigate the risks associated with H-1B layoffs and visa insecurity. While the investment is substantial, the long-term benefits of permanent residency make the EB-5 a valuable proposition for investors. The approaching September 30, 2026 grandfathering deadline is a critical window of opportunity allowing investors to secure their place under the current more favorable program rules. Acting promptly is essential to avoid potential regulatory changes, increased investment amounts and the complexities of future retrogression. The EB-5 program stands as the most robust, future-proof immigration strategy for Indian tech workers to secure a future in the United States.
