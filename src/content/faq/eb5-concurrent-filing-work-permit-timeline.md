EB-5 concurrent filing lets eligible investors who are already in the U.S. file Form I-526E and Form I-485 together, which is the fastest concurrent filing immigration path because it triggers eligibility for an EAD (Form I-765) and Advance Parole (Form I-131) while the underlying petition is still pending. So yes, you can work while EB-5 is pending: once USCIS issues the combo card, you have open-market work authorization that is not tied to any employer, which is why can H-1B holders get work permit through EB-5 concurrent filing is one of the most common questions, and the answer is generally yes when status is maintained. On how long for EB-5 EAD and the EB-5 Advance Parole timeline, processing currently runs roughly 6 to 12 months from the date you file I-526E and I-485 together, and many investors view this as the fastest way to get EB-5 work permit results without leaving the country. Spouses and unmarried children under 21 are included as derivatives on the same I-485, so can spouse work through EB-5 is also yes—your spouse receives an independent EAD with the same open-market flexibility. To file I-526E and I-485 together, your priority date must be current in the relevant set-aside category (rural, high-unemployment, or unreserved), your $800,000 investment must be at risk in a qualifying project, and your source of funds must be fully documented. Consult a qualified immigration attorney for advice specific to your situation.
